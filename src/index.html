Maximising your salary as a Data Scientist

This visualisation presents some factors that affect your salary as a Data Scientist, and suggest ways to maximise your salary based of the findings.

Where to work?

The above choropleth map shows the average salaray (in USD) of a data science job of each country from 2020 to 2022. Grey out countries indicates no data.

The map shows Malaysia has the highest average salary, despite the linecharts below showing otherwise. This is due to Malaysia only having 1 year of data, thus the Malaysian average salary is not comparable with other countries.

The barchart shows Oceania has the highest salary on average, which make sense as both Australia and New Zeeland have respective average salary.

Focusing in the Americas, despite the chart showing it is the second-highest paying region, the average is skewed towards the USA and Canada, the 2 countries with the highest salary on average. Therefore, it is reccomended to work in these 2 nations specifically, but not the America region as a whole.



The above linecharts show 10 countries with the haighest and lowest average salaries across 2020 and 2022. Notice Malaysia is not included in the left line chart, as explained before.

For the 5 Highest Paying Countries, there's an overall decrease in average salary from 2020 to 2022, with Japan suffering the most, dropping from around $150000 in 2020 to around $50000 in 2022. The USA has been the top performer consistenly, with its average salary above/around $150000 for 3 years. Canada and the UK have been holding strong despite the massive dump of Japan and Russia.

For the 5 Lowest Paying Countries, India and Pakistan have a huge growth in average salary from 2021 to 2022. The salary in India even surpassed that of Russia and Japan in 2022.

To maximise the potential salary, the safests bet would be the USA, Canada and the UK, as they all had the least percentage of decrease in average salary, in comparison to other countries.



Does company size and remote ratio of a job affect salary?

Yes, the larger the company, the higher the pay on average.

Some may assume a higher remote ratio of a job correlates a higher average salary, but not really.

The barchart shows jobs with less than 20% of remote and jobs with more than 80% earn more than jobs with remote ratio in between. Jobs with remote ratio greater than 80% pay the highest on average.
